SNAP Benefits
(Food Stamps)
Federal Food Access Free No Immigration Status Check
What this is

SNAP — the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program — gives eligible households a monthly amount of money on a debit card (called an EBT card) to buy groceries at most stores. The amount depends on your household size and income.

In Texas, SNAP is run by the Department of Health and Human Services (HHSC). You can apply online, by mail, or in person at a local HHSC office. Most people hear back within 30 days. If your need is urgent, you may qualify for emergency SNAP within 7 days.

SNAP benefits can be used at most grocery stores, many farmers markets, and some online retailers. They cannot be used for alcohol, tobacco, vitamins, or hot prepared food.

Important: Immigration status is not checked when a US citizen or legal permanent resident applies. Mixed-status families can apply for eligible members. You do not need to report undocumented family members.

Other requirements: You must live in Texas, have a Social Security number, and meet citizenship or immigration status requirements. Most adults between 18–49 without children must work or be in a work program at least 20 hours a week.

How to apply

Online (fastest)

Apply at yourtexasbenefits.com. Takes about 20–30 minutes. You'll need ID, proof of address, income information, and Social Security numbers for household members applying.

In person

Go to any Texas HHSC local office. Harris County has 8 locations. Bring ID, proof of address, and income documents. Bring a Spanish or Vietnamese interpreter if you need one — or ask for one at the office, they are required to provide it.

Get help applying

The Houston Food Bank offers free SNAP enrollment help. You can also call 2-1-1 to be connected to a local navigator.
