Plain-language summary

This bill would expand the number of Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQHCs) in Texas — community health clinics that serve patients regardless of ability to pay, using a sliding-fee scale. Right now, many parts of the Houston metro, especially rural areas in the 18-county region, don't have easy access to an FQHC.

If passed, the bill would direct state funding to support new clinic sites in underserved areas, train more community health workers, and expand the kinds of services these clinics can offer — including mental health and dental care.

The bill does not change insurance coverage rules. It's about putting clinics in more places so people can actually get to one.
