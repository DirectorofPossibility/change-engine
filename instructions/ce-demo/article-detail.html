How to apply for food assistance in Harris County

Community Exchange · March 5, 2026 · 8 min read

If you're having trouble affording food, you have options — and you don't need to be in a crisis to ask for help. This guide covers the main programs available to people in Harris County, what each one offers, who qualifies, and how to apply.

SNAP Benefits (Food Stamps)

SNAP is the federal food assistance program. It puts money on a debit card (called an EBT card) every month that you can use at most grocery stores and farmers markets.

Most households with incomes below 130% of the federal poverty line qualify. For a family of four, that's roughly $3,250 a month before taxes. If your income is higher but you have high housing or childcare costs, you may still qualify.

How to apply: The fastest way is online at yourtexasbenefits.com. It takes about 20–30 minutes. You'll need a Social Security number for each household member who is applying (undocumented family members don't need to apply — and you don't have to report them). If you need help applying, call 2-1-1 or visit a Houston Food Bank SNAP outreach site.

WIC — Women, Infants & Children

WIC is specifically for pregnant women, new mothers, and children under 5. It provides monthly food benefits, nutrition counseling, and breastfeeding support. The income limit is slightly higher than SNAP, so some families who don't qualify for SNAP can still get WIC.

Emergency food — no paperwork required

If you need food right now, you don't need to wait for SNAP or WIC to come through. The Houston Food Bank operates over 1,500 partner sites — food pantries, churches, community centers — where you can get free food with no ID and no income verification required.

To find the closest pantry to you, visit houstonfoodbank.org/find-food or call 2-1-1.

Harris Health Gold Card

If you're uninsured and can't afford medical care, the Harris Health Gold Card gives you access to low-cost primary care, prescriptions, and specialist services at Harris Health System facilities. It's not food assistance, but food insecurity often comes with health issues, and this program connects them.
