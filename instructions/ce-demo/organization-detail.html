About

The Houston Food Bank is the largest food bank in the United States by distribution volume. Founded in 1982, it serves 18 counties across the greater Houston region through a network of over 1,500 partner agencies — food pantries, soup kitchens, shelters, and other community organizations.

In addition to emergency food distribution, the food bank runs SNAP outreach (helping families apply for federal food benefits), nutrition education, workforce training, and a senior food program. In 2024, the organization distributed over 180 million pounds of food.
