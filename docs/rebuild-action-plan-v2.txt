THE CHANGE ENGINE — CIVIC OPERATING SYSTEM REBUILD PLAN
===========================================================
Created: 2026-03-08
Updated: 2026-03-08
Status: PLANNING
Approach: CONTENT ENGINE + CIVIC OS ON-RAMP

The Community Exchange is a civic operating system for Houston.
The frontend is the on-ramp — it helps every resident, regardless of
where they are in their civic journey, find their way into the system.

The content engine ingests civic data, classifies it across 16 dimensions,
braids it along pathways, focus areas, journeys, and geography — and
renders it through templates driven by configuration, not code.

Data in → Classification → Braid → Render.
One pipeline. One engine. Every page is an output.


THE BRAID
---------
The braid is 10,000+ living items woven together:

  Organizations        Nonprofits, mutual aid, government agencies, foundations
  211 Services         Social services — food banks, clinics, shelters, legal aid
  Officials            Every elected official: federal, state, county, city, school board
  Policies             Bills, ordinances, legislation at every level
  News & Content       Articles, reports, videos, research, announcements
  Events               Community events, civic hearings, workshops, elections
  Opportunities        Volunteer, jobs, civic engagement, board seats
  Learning Paths       Courses, curricula, educational journeys
  Guides               Long-form editorial guides by pathway
  Books & Resources    Library documents, toolkits, reading lists
  Civic Tools          "Call Your Senator", "Find Your Polling Place", etc.
  Campaigns            Organized civic campaigns with calls to action
  Candidates           Election candidates with positions, endorsements
  Benefits             Government benefit programs (SNAP, Medicaid, etc.)
  Life Situations      "I need food", "I lost my job" — entry points to resources
  Neighborhoods        Geographic communities with their own civic profile

Organizations are parents — their services, events, opportunities, and
campaigns are children. An organization profile shows everything it offers.
A service profile links back to its parent org and out to related policies,
officials, and content through the braid.

EVERY ITEM gets a full Profile page with wayfinder.
A 211 food bank listing → shows the org that runs it, the council member
for that district, the SNAP policy that funds it, the news article about
food insecurity in that neighborhood, and the "Our Health" pathway it
belongs to. Nothing is a dead end. Everything connects.

Custom tools (like "Call Your Senator") are entities in the braid — they
get classified into pathways and show up in the Stream alongside services,
news, and officials. They are not buttons on a page. They are braided
civic actions.


THE ON-RAMP: 6 JOURNEY ARCHETYPES
-----------------------------------
Every person arrives differently. The archetypes are entry points into
the civic OS, each mapped to a sacred geometry pattern (Flower of Life):

  The Seeker    (Seed of Life)       → "I need something" → Services, benefits
  The Learner   (Vesica Piscis)      → "I want to understand" → Library, guides, Chance
  The Builder   (Tripod of Life)     → "I want to help" → Opportunities, campaigns
  The Watchdog  (Metatron's Cube)    → "Who's in charge?" → Officials, policies, votes
  The Partner   (Borromean Rings)    → "Who's doing this work?" → Organizations, foundations
  The Explorer  (Full Flower)        → "Show me everything" → Pathways, map, calendar

The archetype selection personalizes the entire experience:
  - Stream sections reorder based on archetype priority
  - Wayfinder highlights relevant entity types first
  - Compass journey adapts starting questions
  - Search results weight archetype-relevant types higher

A Seeker sees services and benefits first.
A Watchdog sees officials and policies first.
Same braid, different entry point. Everyone on-ramps their way.


THE 7 PATHWAYS
--------------
Thematic lenses through which all content is organized:

  Our Health          Wellness, clinics, mental health, insurance
  Our Families        Schools, childcare, youth, safety
  Our Neighborhood    Housing, parks, libraries, neighborhood initiatives
  Our Voice           Voting, advocacy, town halls, organizing
  Our Money           Jobs, benefits, credit, small business
  Our Planet          Climate, flooding, energy, sustainability
  The Bigger We       Bridging, dialogue, inclusion, trust

Every entity in the braid belongs to at least one pathway.
Pathways are the browsing experience — the magazine-style Streams.


THE 4 CENTERS
-------------
User-intent modes that cut across all pathways:

  Learning          "How can I understand?"
  Action            "How can I help?"
  Resource          "What's available to me?"
  Accountability    "Who makes decisions?"

Centers filter within a pathway. "Our Health" × "Resource" = clinics,
food banks, insurance programs. "Our Health" × "Accountability" =
health committee members, Medicaid policy, hospital board officials.


FOCUS AREAS
-----------
Granular topics that connect entities across pathways:
  Mental Health, Youth Development, Affordable Housing, Voter Registration,
  Food Access, Climate Resilience, Job Training, Criminal Justice, etc.

Focus areas are the braid threads — they're how a service connects to
a policy connects to an official connects to an article. The 3-hop
graph traversal in wayfinder.ts walks these threads.


THE LIBRARY + CHANCE (AI Assistant)
------------------------------------
Already built. The knowledge base with RAG-powered chat:

  kb_documents     Documents, toolkits, reports, research
  kb_chunks        Chunked + embedded for semantic search
  Chance chatbot   Streams answers from Claude, cites 5 source types
  Bookshelf        Curated reading lists by pathway
  Library nuggets  Excerpt cards that appear in Streams and Profiles

Chance searches across ALL entity types — documents, services, orgs,
content, officials — and answers in 6th-grade reading level with
asset-based language. Chance is the conversational on-ramp.

In the rebuild: Chance becomes a persistent presence, not just a
floating button. The library integrates into the braid — documents
show up as EntityCards alongside services and news in Streams.


THE CALENDAR
------------
Already built. Unified event calendar merging 4 sources:

  Community events      From partner organizations
  Civic calendar        Government meetings, hearings, deadlines
  Opportunities         Volunteer dates, registration deadlines
  Content events        Events surfaced from published content

Month/week/3-day/day views. Filterable by category, pathway, type.
Google Calendar integration, .ics subscribe, registration links.

In the rebuild: The calendar becomes a first-class template (not just
a page) — events appear in Streams, in Profiles (org's upcoming events),
and in the Map (events near you). The calendar view is one lens on
the same braided data.

THE CONTENT ENGINE MODEL
================================================================================

    ┌─────────────┐     ┌──────────────┐     ┌─────────────┐
    │   INGEST    │────▶│   CLASSIFY   │────▶│    STORE     │
    │ APIs, RSS,  │     │ 16 dimensions│     │  Supabase    │
    │ manual URLs │     │ AI pipeline  │     │  + junctions │
    └─────────────┘     └──────────────┘     └──────┬───────┘
                                                     │
                            ┌────────────────────────┘
                            ▼
                     ┌─────────────┐
                     │   BRAID     │
                     │ Query by:   │
                     │ • pathway   │
                     │ • focus area│
                     │ • geography │
                     │ • center    │
                     │ • journey   │
                     └──────┬──────┘
                            │
              ┌─────────────┼─────────────┐
              ▼             ▼             ▼
        ┌──────────┐  ┌──────────┐  ┌──────────┐
        │ TEMPLATE │  │ TEMPLATE │  │ TEMPLATE │
        │ Stream   │  │ Profile  │  │ Explorer │
        └──────────┘  └──────────┘  └──────────┘

    The engine decides WHAT to show based on:
    • Entity type config (what fields, what card variant, what sections)
    • User context (ZIP, language, archetype)
    • Route context (pathway, focus area, entity type)
    • Braid connections (what's related across types)

    The templates decide HOW to show it.
    The content engine decides WHAT and WHY.

PHASE 2: CONTENT ENGINE CORE (Week 1-2)
================================================================================
Goal: Build the engine that drives the entire frontend from config.
This is the brain — every page, card, and connection flows from this.

2.1 — Entity Type Registry
  [ ] Define EntityTypeConfig schema:
      {
        type: string                    // 'official' | 'service' | 'policy' | ...
        table: string                   // Supabase table name
        displayName: string             // 'Elected Official'
        pluralName: string              // 'Elected Officials'
        slug: string                    // URL segment: 'officials'
        icon: string                    // lucide icon name
        color: string                   // default accent color

        // Field mapping — tells the engine where to find data
        fields: {
          id: string                    // column name for primary key
          title: string                 // column for display title
          subtitle: string | null       // column for subtitle (title, org_name, etc.)
          description: string           // column for body text
          image: string | null          // column for image URL
          date: string | null           // column for date (published_at, event_date)
          status: string | null         // column for status (active, enacted, etc.)
          url: string | null            // column for external URL
        }

        // Contact fields — what actions are available
        contacts: {
          email: string | null
          phone: string | null
          website: string | null
          address: string | null
          social: string | null         // linkedin, twitter column
        }

        // Card rendering config
        card: {
          variant: 'compact' | 'standard' | 'featured'
          showImage: boolean
          showBadge: boolean            // type pill
          showPathway: boolean          // pathway dot
          showContact: boolean          // action buttons
          showLevel: boolean            // government level indicator
          showStatus: boolean           // status badge
          imageAspect: 'square' | 'wide' | 'portrait'
        }

        // Detail page config
        detail: {
          sections: string[]            // ordered list of section keys to show
          actions: string[]             // 'email' | 'call' | 'visit' | 'donate' | 'volunteer'
          relatedTypes: string[]        // which entity types to show as related
          showTaxonomy: boolean         // show SDG, SDOH, NTEE badges
          showTimeline: boolean         // show date/status timeline
          showMap: boolean              // show location map
        }

        // Parent-child relationships
        children: {
          types: string[]               // child entity types: ['service', 'event', 'opportunity']
          foreignKey: string            // column on child table: 'org_id'
        } | null

        parent: {
          type: string                  // parent entity type: 'organization'
          foreignKey: string            // column on this table: 'org_id'
        } | null

        // Braid config — how this entity type participates in braiding
        braid: {
          junctionTable: string         // e.g. 'official_focus_areas'
          junctionEntityKey: string     // e.g. 'official_id'
          junctionFocusKey: string      // e.g. 'focus_area_id'
          pathwayJunction: string       // e.g. 'official_pathways'
          geoField: string | null       // 'zip_code' | 'jurisdiction' | null
        }
      }

  [ ] Create config entries for all 16 entity types:
      officials, services, policies, content, organizations,
      events, opportunities, agencies, benefits, foundations,
      candidates, learning-paths, guides, neighborhoods,
      civic-tools, campaigns

  [ ] Define parent-child relationships in registry:

      Opportunity  → parent: organization
      Campaign     → parent: organization

  [ ] Export: ENTITY_REGISTRY: Record<string, EntityTypeConfig>
  [ ] Export: getEntityConfig(type: string): EntityTypeConfig
  [ ] Export: getEntityField(type, entity, fieldName): any

2.2 — Braid Engine
  [ ] buildBraidedFeed(options):
      Input:  { pathway?, focusArea?, zip?, center?, types?, limit, sort }
      Output: BraidedEntity[] (mixed, typed, sorted)
      Logic:  Uses entity registry to query correct tables + junctions
              Merges results, normalizes to BraidedEntity shape
              Scores by relevance (recency + connection density + geo proximity)

  [ ] getEntityProfile(type, id):
      Input:  entity type + id
      Output: { entity, connections, related, actions, taxonomy, geo, children, parent }
      Logic:  Reads entity config → fetches correct table
              Wraps getWayfinderContext() for connections
              If config.children → fetch child entities grouped by type
              If config.parent → fetch parent entity for breadcrumb/link
              Applies detail.sections config to determine response shape

  [ ] getStreamSections(pathway?, zip?, archetype?):
      Input:  pathway slug (or null for homepage) + optional zip + archetype
      Output: StreamSection[] (ordered sections for the Stream template)
      Logic:  "What's New" → latest across all types
              Focus area clusters → group by shared focus area
              Journey steps → life situations with ordered resources
              Your reps → officials filtered by zip
              Upcoming events → next 5 from unified calendar
              Library picks → relevant documents/nuggets
              Archetype reorders sections:
                Seeker    → services, benefits, life situations first
                Learner   → library, guides, learning paths first
                Builder   → opportunities, campaigns, events first
                Watchdog  → officials, policies, votes first
                Partner   → organizations, foundations first
                Explorer  → everything evenly weighted
              Each section is a { type, title, entities: BraidedEntity[] }

  [ ] getExplorerData(entityType, filters?):
      Input:  entity type slug + { pathway?, center?, search?, zip?, page }
      Output: { entities: BraidedEntity[], total, filters, facets }
      Logic:  Reads entity config → builds query with filters
              Returns facet counts for filter UI

  [ ] getMapContext(zip?, bounds?, layers?):
      Input:  zip or map bounds + active layer IDs
      Output: { markers, areaCounts, selectedFeed: BraidedEntity[] }

2.3 — BraidedEntity Type
  [ ] Define normalized entity shape:
      {
        entityType: string              // discriminant
        id: string
        title: string
        subtitle: string | null
        description: string
        imageUrl: string | null
        imageFallback: { type, color, letter }
        date: string | null
        status: string | null
        pathway: { id, name, color } | null
        level: { name, color } | null
        contacts: { email?, phone?, website?, address? }

        url: string                     // internal route

        geo: { zip?, neighborhood?, district? } | null
        parentId: string | null         // org_id, election_id, etc.
        parentType: string | null       // 'organization', 'election', etc.
        childCount: number | null       // number of children (services, events, etc.)
      }

THE CIVIC OPERATING SYSTEM
================================================================================

This is not a website. It is a concierge for civic life.

The Russian doll:
  Pathways      → the big themes (health, families, money, voice...)
  Centers       → the intent (learn, act, find resources, hold accountable)
  Focus Areas   → the threads that connect everything
  Organizations → the hubs, with their children (services, events, opportunities)
  Every item    → a full page, braided to everything related

The on-ramp:
  6 archetypes meet people where they are
  Chance (AI) answers questions in plain language
  Everything written at 6th-grade level
  3 languages (EN, ES, VI)
  ZIP code personalizes from first visit

The engine:
  Data in → AI classifies across 16 dimensions → Braid connects everything →
  Templates render based on config → Middleware personalizes at the edge

The old frontend asks: "What page am I on?"
The civic OS asks: "Who are you, what do you need, and what's connected to it?"

Policy gets rewritten so people can understand it.
Services get connected to the officials who fund them.
Organizations show everything they offer.
Events show up everywhere they're relevant.
The library surfaces knowledge alongside action.
Chance explains anything you don't understand.

Every resident can on-ramp into their civic life,
through whichever door makes sense to them.

That's the Community Exchange.
That's the Content Engine.
That's the civic operating system for Houston.
