Health Workers Are Changing Primary Care in Houston

~3 min read / Community health / Updated Mar 2026

If you've been to a clinic recently, you might have talked to someone who wasn't a doctor or a nurse -- but who still really helped. That person was probably a community health worker.

Community health workers (CHWs) are people from your neighborhood who are trained to help connect you to the care you need. They speak your language, understand your situation, and can walk you through things like signing up for insurance, finding a food pantry, or getting your kids into a program.

They help people who don't know where to start. If you've ever thought "I know I need help but I don't know who to call" -- that's exactly who health workers are for.

In Houston, organizations like Legacy Community Health and The Harris Center have health workers on staff. They can visit your home, meet you at a clinic, or talk on the phone.
