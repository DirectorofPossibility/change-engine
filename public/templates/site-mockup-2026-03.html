Houston Launches Free Community Health Screening Program

Preventive health screenings will be available at 15 community centers across the city starting March 2026.

houstonchronicle.com / March 3, 2026
Visit source

The City of Houston, in partnership with Harris Health System and local community organizations, is launching a comprehensive preventive health screening initiative targeting 15 underserved neighborhoods.

Residents can receive free blood pressure checks, diabetes screening, cholesterol testing, and wellness consultations at participating community centers. No appointment or insurance is needed.

The program prioritizes neighborhoods with the highest rates of chronic disease and lowest access to preventive care, including Fifth Ward, Third Ward, Sunnyside, and Acres Homes.

"Preventive care saves lives and reduces healthcare costs," said Houston Health Department Director Dr. Maria Gonzalez. "By bringing screenings directly to communities, we're removing barriers to early detection."

About

The Houston Food Bank is the nation's largest food bank in distribution, providing food and resources to those in need across 18 counties in southeast Texas. Through a network of 1,500+ community partners, the organization serves over 800,000 individuals annually with food assistance, nutrition education, and benefit enrollment support.

Services (3)

Food Distribution

Weekly food distribution at community partner sites across the 18-county service area.

Nutrition Education

Free cooking classes and nutrition workshops for families and seniors.

Benefits Enrollment

Help applying for SNAP, WIC, and other government food assistance programs.
