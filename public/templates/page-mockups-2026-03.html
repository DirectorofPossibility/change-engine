Colorado expands naloxone access to fight maternal overdose deaths

goodgoodgood.co · Mar 4, 2026
📎 Read original source 🤝 Volunteer 📞 Call 📝 Sign up
1

New state law makes naloxone available without prescription

Colorado passed a law that lets pharmacies give out naloxone to anyone who asks. This medicine can reverse an opioid overdose and has already saved thousands of lives nationwide.

2

Why this matters for families

Pregnant women face a higher risk of overdose. Having naloxone nearby saves lives. The new law means families can keep this life-saving medication in their homes without navigating complex medical systems.

3

What you can do

  • Ask your local pharmacy if they carry naloxone
  • Share this information with family and friends
  • Contact your representative to support similar Texas legislation

Programs & Initiatives

CO Naloxone Program

State-funded distribution of naloxone kits to pharmacies and community orgs.

SAMHSA Grant Program

Federal grants supporting overdose prevention and recovery services.

Why was this classified here?

This article discusses naloxone policy, a substance abuse and maternal health topic. It maps to Our Health pathway, Learning center, with focus areas in Substance Abuse, Maternal Health, and Pharmacy Access.
