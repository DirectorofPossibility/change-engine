ouston has world-class medical institutions and some of the worst health outcomes of any major American city. Both things are true. The Texas Medical Center — the largest in the world — sits inside a city where nearly a million residents have no health insurance, where the maternal mortality rate for Black women is three times the rate for white women, and where zip code remains the strongest predictor of how long you'll live.

Uninsured residents in Harris County

The highest uninsured rate of any major metro county. Most are working adults in industries without employer coverage. Federal marketplace enrollment has grown, but the gap remains enormous.

1 in 4
Houston children experience food insecurity

Houston has more food banks and food pantries per capita than most cities its size — and still ranks near the bottom for food security outcomes. Distribution, awareness, and access barriers are all at work.

3×
Gap in maternal mortality by race

Black mothers in Harris County die at more than three times the rate of white mothers during or after childbirth. Doula access, insurance coverage, and hospital proximity are all factors with active advocacy campaigns.

About this organization
What the Harris Center actually is — and who it's for

The Harris Center is the local mental health authority for Harris County — which means it's the government-designated organization responsible for providing publicly funded mental health and intellectual/developmental disability services in the region. That sounds bureaucratic. In practice, it means it's the place you go when other systems haven't worked, when you can't afford private care, or when you're in crisis and need help immediately.

The Harris Center does not turn people away based on ability to pay. Services are available on a sliding-fee scale, and they accept Medicaid, CHIP, and many insurance plans. For uninsured residents, costs are based on household income. That's not marketing language — it's the actual policy.

They operate ten outpatient locations across Harris County, a 24-hour crisis stabilization unit, mobile crisis outreach teams, and a crisis hotline staffed by mental health professionals. They also run one of the largest jail diversion programs in Texas — which means they're the organization keeping mentally ill residents out of the criminal justice system.

Services available
Outpatient Mental Health Counseling
Individual and group therapy, psychiatric evaluation, medication management. Sliding scale.
24/7 Crisis Services
Crisis hotline, mobile outreach teams, crisis stabilization unit. No appointment needed.
Intellectual & Developmental Disability Services
Case management, residential support, day programs for adults and children.
Jail Diversion Program
Mental health intervention for people entering the Harris County criminal justice system.
